Our Story

From a Single Gathering to a Statewide Movement

Inspired by Dr. Isaac Pope of Pope's Kids Place, the Lewis County Autism Coalition was founded in 2010 through a state-led Community Asset Mapping Project. From that first gathering of parents and providers, a movement took root -- grounded in collaboration, belonging, and action. Now a longstanding 501(c)(3) nonprofit, LCAC continues to champion belonging for autistic, neurodiverse, and developmentally disabled individuals across Washington State.

What We Do

Programs Across a Lifetime

LCAC is best known for our annual Southwest Washington Autism Conference and for developing the SMART (School-Medical Autism Review Team) model — now active in 31 Washington counties. Beyond that, we run inclusive, year-round programming for autistic, neurodiverse, and developmentally disabled individuals and their families.

Neurodiverse Teen & Adult Social Groups

Year-round connection groups where teens and adults on the spectrum build friendships, develop skills, and find community.

ASD Groups of Belonging

Support groups designed to create safe, welcoming spaces for autistic individuals and the people who love them.

Summer Spectrum Camp

A sensory-friendly summer experience for kids — with activities designed around how every camper learns, plays, and connects.

Cultivating Inclusion Celebration

Our annual celebration honoring individuals and organizations who go the extra mile to make Lewis County more welcoming for people of all abilities.

Milestones

Key Achievements

Every milestone reflects our community's commitment to building a more welcoming world, one step at a time.

🧵

Weighted Warmth Program

Providing free compression and weighted vests to individuals who benefit from deep-pressure sensory input -- removing cost as a barrier to comfort.

💰

Micro-Grant Program

Supporting schools and community organizations with targeted funding to advance autism-friendly and welcoming initiatives at the local level.

🚗

2nd Annual Car & BMX Show (2025)

A community-wide celebration featuring cars, BMX, and family fun -- raising awareness and support while bringing people together in a welcoming setting.

🏠

Spectrum & Development Community Center

The grand opening of a 5,000 sq ft community-built space in Napavine -- a home for programs, services, and connection, co-located with Lewis County Pediatrics & Family Medicine.

Our Mission

The Lewis County Autism Coalition remains committed to building a more welcoming future for autistic, neurodiverse, and developmentally disabled individuals -- locally and beyond. Rooted in strong partnerships and grassroots leadership, we work every day to ensure that everyone has the opportunity to belong, grow, and thrive.

LCAC Leadership

Meet the board and staff guiding our mission to build a community where everyone belongs.

Executive Director

Michelle M. Whitlow, M.S.

executivedirector@lcautism.org

It is a privilege to connect with you and share my vision for the exciting future we can create together. As the Executive Director of the Lewis County Autism Coalition, I bring over two decades of experience in advocating for underserved populations and a specialized master's education in leadership and community interventions and a strong background in research. Most importantly, I am driven by a deep passion for serving our community and collaborating with each of you.

Our coalition is dedicated to building a compassionate and supportive community where individuals with autism, developmental disabilities, and neurodiverse needs — along with their families — can access the resources and services they need to thrive. With your support, we are working to remove barriers to care, expand opportunities, and foster meaningful connections that benefit everyone.

We are embarking on transformative initiatives to strengthen our impact. This includes enhancing beloved programs like our sensory-friendly summer camps and neurodiverse social groups, as well as introducing new projects tailored to meet the unique needs of our region. A cornerstone of these efforts is our new Spectrum & Development Community Center. This innovative space serves as a dynamic hub, offering varied services all designed to empower rural families and individuals with autism and developmental disabilities.

In addition to expanding our programs, we thrive on collaborative partnerships with businesses, schools, and community organizations. These relationships are essential to creating a supportive environment where every individual is empowered to contribute and succeed.

Warm regards,
Michelle Whitlow, M.S.
Executive Director, Lewis County Autism Coalition

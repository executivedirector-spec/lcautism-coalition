For Attorneys & Public Defenders

Court & Systems Support

Autism-Informed Family Support & Care Coordination for OPD-Involved Cases

Program Overview

What We Do

Lewis County Autism Coalition provides autism-informed, non-clinical family support and care coordination services to attorneys representing children and parents in dependency and related proceedings. These services are designed to support legal strategy, improve system coordination, and reduce misinterpretation of disability-related needs.

What's Included

Services Provided

Six core service areas designed to support attorneys, families, and the systems involved.

Autism-Informed Family Education and Stabilization Support

Direct support for families navigating dependency proceedings, with attention to autism-related communication, regulation, and routine needs.

SMART Services

School-Medical-Autism-Review-Team coordination to align all involved professionals around shared, autism-aware case strategy.

Bilingual (English/Spanish) Care Coordination and Communication

Direct, culturally responsive support for Spanish-speaking families, in plain language, throughout the case.

Cross-System Coordination

Active coordination across education, medical, child welfare, therapeutic, and multiple service providers â€” so the case has one shared picture, not five fragmented ones.

Attorney Consultation with Comprehensive, Written Reference Summaries

Plain-English written summaries that translate autism-specific information into clear, useable reference material for legal strategy.

School and IEP Consultation Support

Coordination with school staff and IEP teams to ensure educational plans are aligned with the child's needs and the broader case context.

Support is also available in cases involving tribal (ICWA) or international coordination.

To Be Clear

Scope Boundaries

What LCAC does not provide.

We do NOT provide clinical diagnosis or treatment

Although we can connect you to those services.

We do NOT provide legal advice

Legal strategy and advocacy remain entirely with the representing attorney.

We do NOT provide forensic evaluations or expert testimony unless separately contracted

These services are out of scope for standard family support engagements.

How Engagements Work

Authorization & Billing

LCAC services for court-involved cases begin only after written authorization is received from the Office of Public Defense or another identified funding source. Services are contracted directly with the representing attorney and billed in accordance with approval parameters.

Get Started

How to Access Services

Attorneys and other professionals seeking LCAC support for an OPD-involved case should complete our brief intake form. The form helps us appropriately scope services and support child welfare authorization requests.

Completion of the form does not initiate services. Services begin only after written authorization is received.

Intake / Authorization Form Request Services

Have Questions Before Filing?

Reach out before submitting your intake form. We're happy to walk you through scope, authorization, and what to expect.
